I am a Research Scientist at Samsung AI Center-Cambridge working with Dr Stylianos I. Venieris and a Visiting Scholar at the University of Cambridge. I received my PhD from the University of Cambridge under Prof. Cecilia Mascolo. My research makes large generative models run efficiently on resource-constrained devices and ships them to millions of users, spanning on-device LLM acceleration, diffusion model compression, and continual adaptation through system-algorithm co-design.

I have delivered three consecutive On-Device AI Commercialisations on Samsung Galaxy flagship devices: LLM acceleration via speculative decoding (S26), diffusion-based super-resolution (S25), and the industry's first dynamic AI wallpaper (S24), shipped across seven device lines (S24-S26, Z Flip/Fold 6-7) and featured at Galaxy Unpacked 2024 (tens of millions of views) with showcase videos generating 400K+ views (YouTube (KR) | Shorts (EN)).

Recently recognised as an ACM MobiSys Rising Star (2026) and MLCommons ML & Systems Rising Star (2025) , my work has earned consecutive Samsung Best Paper Silver Awards (2024 & 2025) and Best Paper Award at SEC '21, with 30+ publications across top AI (ICML, NeurIPS, ICLR, and AAAI) and systems (SenSys, IPSN, TMC, MobiCom, and IMWUT) venues. I also serve as PC Chair for EMDL 2026 at MobiSys and have mentored 17+ students across PhD, MPhil, and undergraduate levels, producing 9+ publications, including at ICLR, NeurIPS, and ACL.

Research Focus:

  • Efficient Generative AI Systems: Reimagining resource-intensive generative models for mobile and edge devices
  • On-Device Training & Continual Adaptation: Enabling devices to learn locally with minimal data, preserving privacy and improving personalisation
  • System-Algorithm Co-Design for IoT: Creating optimised solutions that synergise hardware capabilities and algorithmic innovations for resource-constrained environments
